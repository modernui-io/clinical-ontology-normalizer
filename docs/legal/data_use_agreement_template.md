## 3. Definitions

- **PHI (Protected Health Information):** Individually identifiable health information as defined by HIPAA.
- **De-Identified Data:** Data that has been stripped of all 18 HIPAA identifiers.
- **Limited Data Set:** PHI that excludes direct identifiers but may include dates, geographic data, and other indirect identifiers.
- **Aggregate Data:** Statistical summaries that cannot identify individuals.
- **Clinical Trial Data:** Data generated during or in support of clinical trial activities.

## 7. Data Security Requirements

The Recipient agrees to:

1. Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards per HIPAA Security Rule
2. Encrypt data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+)
3. Restrict access to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis
4. Maintain audit logs of all data access
5. Conduct annual security risk assessments
6. Report any security incidents within 24 hours of discovery
7. Ensure all personnel complete HIPAA training before accessing data

## 8. Data Retention and Destruction

1. **Retention Period:** Data shall be retained for [N] days from the effective date
2. **Clinical Trial Data:** Minimum 6 years per 21 CFR Part 11
3. **Destruction:** Upon expiration or termination, Recipient shall:
   - Destroy all copies of the data within 30 days
   - Certify destruction in writing to Provider
   - Retain only the minimum necessary for legal compliance

## 9. Breach Notification

1. Recipient shall notify Provider within 24 hours of discovering a breach
2. Notification shall include: nature of breach, data involved, individuals affected, remedial actions
3. Recipient shall cooperate with Provider in breach response and notification to affected individuals
4. Costs of breach response shall be borne by the party responsible for the breach

## 10. Amendments

1. This Agreement may be amended only in writing signed by both parties
2. Amendments to active agreements require documented justification
3. Amendment history shall be maintained as part of the agreement record

## 11. Term and Termination

1. This Agreement is effective from the Effective Date through the Expiration Date
2. Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice
3. Termination does not relieve obligations regarding data already received
4. Data destruction obligations survive termination

## 12. Compliance

1. Both parties shall comply with HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules
2. Both parties shall comply with applicable state privacy laws
3. For clinical trial data: compliance with 21 CFR Part 11 and ICH GCP
4. Both parties shall cooperate with audits and compliance reviews

## 13. Indemnification

Each party shall indemnify the other against claims arising from the indemnifying party's breach of this Agreement or violation of applicable law.

## DUA Type-Specific Addenda

### A. Site DUA Addendum

Additional terms for agreements between the platform and clinical trial sites:
- Site shall obtain patient consent before sharing PHI
- Site retains ownership of patient data
- Platform access limited to screening and enrollment workflows

### B. Sponsor DUA Addendum

Additional terms for agreements between the platform and trial sponsors:
- Sponsor receives only de-identified or aggregate data by default
- Any PHI access requires separate HIPAA authorization
- Sponsor reporting limited to agreed metrics

### C. Research DUA Addendum

Additional terms for secondary research use:
- IRB approval required before data access
- Publications must use only aggregate findings
- Research team must be listed and approved

### D. Vendor DUA Addendum

Additional terms for third-party service providers:
- Vendor acts as Business Associate under HIPAA
- Subcontracting requires prior written approval
- Data must be returned or destroyed at contract end
- Vendor must maintain SOC 2 Type II certification
