## Executive Summary

Regeneron Pharmaceuticals is executing one of the most ambitious clinical development programs in the industry, with **~45 product candidates in clinical development**, plans to initiate **18 new Phase 3 studies in 2026**, and **R&D spending of $5.9-6.1 billion** in 2026. The company expects **at least 4 FDA approvals** in 2026 for new molecular entities plus the EYLEA HD prefilled syringe.

Full-year 2025 revenues reached $14.3B, with Dupixent global net sales at $17.8B (+26% YoY). The company's expanding late-stage pipeline creates significant demand for efficient trial enrollment and site-level patient screening across ophthalmology, immunology, oncology, rare disease, cardiology, and allergy.

### Ophthalmology (EYLEA / EYLEA HD)
- EYLEA HD approved for wAMD, DME, DR (2023), and RVO (Nov 2025)
- EYLEA HD U.S. net sales grew 66% YoY in Q4 2025 ($1.1B quarterly)
- Full-year EYLEA HD U.S. net sales: $1.6B (+36% YoY)
- Presentations at Angiogenesis 2026 conference (Feb 2026)
- EYLEA HD prefilled syringe approval expected 2026
- Trial footprint: PULSAR, PHOTON, QUASAR studies completed (large multi-center global trials)

### Immunology / Inflammation (Dupixent)
- Dupixent is the world's top-selling immunology drug: $17.8B global net sales in 2025 (+26%)
- Studied in 60+ clinical trials involving 10,000+ patients across type 2 inflammatory diseases
- Currently approved: atopic dermatitis, asthma, CRSwNP, EoE, prurigo nodularis, COPD, chronic spontaneous urticaria (EU), bullous pemphigoid
- Upcoming regulatory decisions: AFRS (PDUFA Feb 28, 2026), BP (Priority Review)
- Ongoing expansion into additional type 2 inflammatory indications

### Oncology
- **LIBTAYO (cemiplimab):** Approved in CSCC (including adjuvant, Oct 2025), BCC, NSCLC; +13% growth in Q4 2025
- **Fianlimab (anti-LAG-3):** Two Phase 3 melanoma trials recruiting (Harmony program)
- **Linvoseltamab (BCMAxCD3 bispecific):** BLA under Priority Review for R/R MM (PDUFA July 2025); 8 pivotal studies planned by mid-2026 across myeloma treatment continuum
- **Odronextamab (CD20xCD3 bispecific):** BLA for R/R FL (regulatory challenges with manufacturing site)

### Rare Disease
- **Garetosmab (FOP):** Phase 3 OPTIMA trial positive; >99% reduction in abnormal bone formation; regulatory filings 2025-2026
- Ultra-rare indication (~5,000-8,000 patients worldwide)

### Cardiology / Anticoagulation
- **Factor XI antibodies (REGN7508 + REGN9933):** Next-generation anticoagulants
- Phase 2 results: REGN7508 superior to enoxaparin, non-inferior to apixaban, with no clinically relevant bleeding
- Multiple Phase 3 trials planned 2026: stroke prevention in A-fib, post-surgical DVT
- Potentially transformative: addresses $10B+ anticoagulant market

### Obesity / Metabolic
- **COURAGE trial (trevogrumab +/- garetosmab + semaglutide):**
  - Trevogrumab preserved 50-80% of lean mass lost during GLP-1 therapy
  - Triplet combination (semaglutide + trevogrumab + garetosmab) preserved 80.9% of lean mass
  - Addresses key GLP-1 concern: muscle wasting during weight loss
  - Phase 3 expected to follow; completion of Phase 2 late 2026

### Allergy (First-in-Class Allergen Blockers)
- Cat allergy (REGN1908/1909): 52% reduction in ocular itching; 65% in Fel d 1-driven allergies
- Birch allergy (REGN5713/5715): 51% reduction in ocular itching
- Further Phase 3 development: birch (late 2025), cat (H1 2026)
- Novel approach: direct allergen blockade rather than immune modulation

## Business Context: Why Regeneron Needs Better Site-Level Screening

### Scale of the Challenge
- **18 new Phase 3 trials in 2026** targeting ~35,000 cumulative patients
- **~45 active clinical programs** simultaneously
- **$5.9-6.1B R&D spend** in 2026 -- every enrollment delay burns significant capital
- **$7B in U.S. capital expenditures** for manufacturing and research facilities

### Enrollment Pain Points
1. **Diverse therapeutic areas:** Ophthalmology, immunology, oncology, rare disease, cardiology, allergy, obesity -- each requires different site expertise and patient populations
2. **Biomarker-driven enrollment:** Many trials require specific biomarkers (e.g., blood eosinophils >=300 for COPD, Fel d 1-driven allergy) leading to high screen failure rates
3. **Rare disease trials:** FOP (~5,000-8,000 patients worldwide) and other ultra-rare indications require specialized global site networks
4. **Competitive enrollment:** Multiple myeloma, melanoma, and NSCLC trials compete with dozens of other sponsors for the same patient populations
5. **Manufacturing/regulatory coupling:** Odronextamab BLA was delayed by FDA manufacturing site inspection issues -- site-level quality is critical beyond just enrollment
6. **GLP-1 combination trials:** The COURAGE obesity program must co-enroll patients already on semaglutide, adding screening complexity

### Screening Failure Patterns (Industry Data Applicable to Regeneron)
- Typical Phase 3 screen failure rates: 20-40%
- Biomarker-stratified trials (like Dupixent COPD, eosinophil cutoff): 30-50% screen failures
- Oncology trials with specific molecular markers: up to 60% screen failures
- Common reasons: inclusion/exclusion criteria not met, comorbidities, concomitant medications, lab values out of range, patient withdrawal of consent

### Opportunity for Our Platform
- **Pre-screening optimization:** NLP-driven analysis of EHR data to identify eligible patients before formal screening
- **Site performance analytics:** Predict which sites will meet enrollment targets based on patient population characteristics
- **Protocol feasibility:** Match protocol criteria against real-world patient data to estimate realistic enrollment timelines
- **Biomarker pre-qualification:** Flag patients likely to meet biomarker thresholds before expensive screening visits
- **Cross-trial deconfliction:** With 45+ active programs, identify patients who may be eligible for multiple trials and optimize site-level trial allocation
